Our Task

Our task for this machine learning project is to predict a congressman’s political party based on their tweets. This subject is particularly timely because, as the trends of Russian bots and fake news have demonstrated in the past few years, social media is a powerful medium for impacting the minds of voters. Therefore, the use of social media is essential for today’s politicians to gain an edge in elections by creating a medium in which they can connect with their constituents and receive feedback, demonstrate their commitment to making a difference for their voter base, and to give their views on current events, bills, elections, etc. Twitter is also a very unique medium when it comes to expression because the limited word count in each tweet forces the user to make each word count; therefore, the words that the politician use in each tweet are more likely to be more partisan to appeal to their voter base.

Our Approach

The task is a text classification problem, so Support Vector Machines and Naive Bayes were our best options. Our standard, baseline features consisted of all the words of all the tweets for each congressman. We varied the features by filtering them in different ways, such as only using hashtags and only using @'s.

Key Results

We saw extremely high accuracies in both our classifiers. On our standard features, SVM had a 5-fold cross-validation accuracy of 95% and Naive Bayes an accuracy of 93%. Only using hashtags saw an even greater performance in Naive Bayes, reaching 95% and the same accuracy for SVM. In our report, we discuss in more detail how varying the features affected the accuracies.
